About Us

We are Hopkins Sport Taekwondo Club (HST), a sports club team at Johns Hopkins University that practices and competes in the World Taekwondo (WT, formerly WTF) style of taekwondo. Our team is led by a WT-certified master from our parent school of the U.S. Taekwondo Academy (USTA), Master JY Kim. Our members range from beginners to black belts and test frequently to move up in belt rank. We offer free practices on campus and opportunities to compete regionally and nationally. As a member of our team, you can experience sparring and forms in both practice and competition. We are excited for you to join us and look forward to our time together!

Affiliations

  • World Taekwondo

    HST practices World Taekwondo (WT) style sparring and poomsae. Kukkiwon is the headquarters of the World Taekwondo and is located in Seoul, Korea. The World Taekwondo rules and regulations are the leading form of taekwondo and are used during the Olympic Games.

    More information about WT can be found here.

  • Eastern Collegiate Taekwondo Conference

    Hopkins Sport Taekwondo is a yearly member of the Eastern Collegiate Taekwondo Conference, which hosts tournaments throughout the year at MIT, Brown, Cornell, Princeton, and the University of Vermont. Each tournament boasts around 350 collegiate competitors in sparring and poomsae.

    The tournament rules can be found here on the ECTC website.

Testing

The masters conduct belt tests twice a semester. In order to test, members must have attended at least two-thirds of all weekday practices (Mon, Wed) subsequent to the prior belt test. All HST members are strongly encouraged to test. Here are some resources for testing:

Participants are tested in the following areas:

1. General fitness: push-ups and sit-ups
2. Punching
3. Kicking
4. Forms
5. Defense
6. Terminology
7. Sparring and board breaking (red and above)
